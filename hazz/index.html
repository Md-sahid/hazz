Frequently Asked Questions

Umrah is a pilgrimage to Mecca that can be undertaken at any time of the year. Unlike Hajj, which has specific dates, Umrah is a lesser pilgrimage that holds significant spiritual merit in Islam.

While both Umrah and Hajj are pilgrimages to Mecca, Hajj is obligatory for all Muslims at least once in their lifetime if they are physically and financially able. Hajj has specific dates and rituals, while Umrah can be performed at any time and is considered optional

Yes, you need an Umrah visa to enter Saudi Arabia for the pilgrimage. Our website provides information on how to obtain the necessary visa and documentation.

The main rituals of Umrah include:

  • Ihram: Entering a state of spiritual purity by wearing specific garments
  • Tawaf: Circling the Kaaba seven times in a counterclockwise direction.
  • Sa’i: Walking seven times between the hills of Safa and Marwah.
  • Halq or Taqsir: Shaving the head or cutting a portion of hair to signify the completion of Umrah

Umrah can be performed at any time of the year, but many pilgrims prefer to visit during the months of Ramadan for the added spiritual rewards. However, travel during off-peak seasons can also offer a less crowded experience.
